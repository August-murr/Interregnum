# UBI Is a Trap

### The AI Resource Curse

A resource curse occurs when a society becomes so dependent on a valuable resource that its institutions begin to decay. Governments rely less on people and more on the resource itself, reducing incentives to invest in education, healthcare, democratic participation, or economic opportunity.

AI and automation risk creating the ultimate resource curse.

For most of history, wealth depended on human labor. Even exploitative systems required people to work, produce, fight, invent, and consume. But a sufficiently automated economy breaks that relationship.

If machines produce most economic value, human beings become less economically necessary.

## Worth Less Than Slaves

This is not a return to slavery or feudalism.

Slaves and peasants still had economic value. They worked the fields, built infrastructure, and produced goods.

In a highly automated economy, the majority may not even be needed for production.

The danger is not that people become slaves.

The danger is that they become economically irrelevant.

## Who's Going to Buy All the Stuff?

A common objection is simple:

> If unemployment becomes widespread, who will buy all the products?

The answer may be uncomfortable.

For most of human history, mass consumerism did not exist.

Economic activity was concentrated among a relatively small elite that traded wealth, services, luxury goods, land, and influence among themselves while the majority lived at subsistence levels.

A future automated economy could move in the same direction.

Luxury brands continue selling to wealthy consumers. High-end services continue serving wealthy clients. Capital owners trade with other capital owners.

The economy does not necessarily collapse.

It simply restructures around a smaller consumer base.

Businesses built around serving the mass market may stagnate, disappear, or reinvent themselves to serve the top of the economic pyramid.

## UBI as Hush Money

In this scenario, Universal Basic Income becomes politically attractive.

Not because society is committed to shared prosperity.

Because social stability has value.

UBI funded through AI or automation taxes could provide just enough income to prevent unrest while leaving the underlying concentration of wealth and power untouched.

The promise is that AI will make everyone richer.

The risk is that AI makes a small minority vastly richer while everyone else receives a maintenance payment.

Enough to survive.

Not enough to participate meaningfully in economic life.

UBI may not become a mechanism for distributing AI's benefits.

It may become a mechanism for managing the consequences of excluding people from them.

## The Democratic Risk

Modern democracies depend on people being economically relevant.

Workers, consumers, taxpayers, and voters all possess leverage because society depends on them.

If governments and corporations derive most of their wealth from automated systems rather than human activity, that relationship weakens.

A world that relies less on human labor may also rely less on human consent.

That is the true AI resource curse.

Not mass unemployment.

The possibility that economic dependence on people disappears altogether.
