# Intelligence Without Borders

### A framework for inclusive global AI governance

## Summary

Artificial intelligence is becoming a foundational technology for economic growth, scientific progress, and public decision-making. Yet its development is increasingly concentrated within a small number of countries and corporations.

This concentration risks creating a future where the benefits, standards, and governance of AI are determined by a handful of actors rather than the global community.

**Intelligence Without Borders (IWB)** is a proposed independent, international organization dedicated to ensuring that AI serves humanity as a whole. Its mission is to promote inclusive participation in AI governance, expand access to AI capabilities, and prevent the long-term concentration of technological power.

## The Challenge

Today, the global AI landscape is shaped largely by geopolitical competition, national security priorities, and unequal access to compute, talent, and capital.

As a result:

* A small number of nations have disproportionate influence over the future of AI.
* Many countries have little meaningful participation in AI governance despite being affected by its outcomes.
* Access to advanced AI capabilities risks becoming a source of global inequality rather than shared prosperity.

The question is not who leads AI, but whether the benefits of AI will be broadly distributed or concentrated among a few powerful actors.

## Our Mission

Intelligence Without Borders seeks to strengthen the voice and participation of nations and communities that are currently underrepresented in AI governance.

Key areas of work include:

1. **Inclusive AI Governance**
   Developing frameworks that give all nations a meaningful role in shaping the rules and priorities surrounding advanced AI.

2. **Global Participation Mechanisms**
   Researching and testing systems that allow people across countries to contribute to decisions about the deployment and oversight of powerful AI systems.

3. **Computational Equity**
   Exploring ways to expand access to AI infrastructure, research, and education beyond a small number of technology hubs.

4. **Cross-Border Cooperation**
   Building partnerships between governments, researchers, civil society organizations, and communities to promote shared standards and mutual accountability.

## Principles

* AI should benefit all people, not only the most powerful states or corporations.
* Participation in AI governance should be global and inclusive.
* Transparency and accountability are essential to legitimate AI systems.
* Concentrations of technological power should be treated as a governance risk.
* International cooperation is preferable to technological nationalism.

## Conclusion

Artificial intelligence will shape the future of every nation, not just those currently leading its development.

The goal of Intelligence Without Borders is simple: to ensure that the benefits, opportunities, and governance of AI are shared globally rather than concentrated in the hands of a few.

A technology with global consequences requires institutions with a global perspective.
